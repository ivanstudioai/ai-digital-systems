The insight suggests that overwhelm arises when established lines between effort, skill, and results become blurred. When outcomes no longer clearly reflect the time or expertise invested, it becomes difficult to interpret one’s own contribution or value. This uncertainty disrupts familiar ways of making sense of work, progress, and decision-making. What feels exhausting is not the pace of change itself, but the absence of stable reference points. That matters because orientation is a quiet prerequisite for confidence, trust, and meaningful judgment. In environments where roles, tools, or capabilities shift faster than shared understanding, this kind of disorientation can spread even without obvious failure. The insight helps explain why reactions often appear emotional or existential rather than purely technical.
