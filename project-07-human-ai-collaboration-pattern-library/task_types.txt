TASK TYPES

This file defines common categories of knowledge work where human–AI
collaboration can be structured.

Task types are intentionally broad and stable. They represent recurring
forms of work rather than specific tools or prompts.

---

TASK TYPE: Strategic

Description:
Work focused on direction, planning, and decision framing under uncertainty.

Strategic tasks typically involve defining goals, exploring options,
and reasoning about future outcomes.

Typical Examples:

* Product strategy
* Business planning
* Roadmap design
* Market positioning
* Project direction setting

Primary Challenge:
High ambiguity and incomplete information.

---

TASK TYPE: Analytical

Description:
Work focused on understanding systems, information, or problems through
structured reasoning.

Analytical tasks involve breaking down complexity, identifying patterns,
and explaining relationships.

Typical Examples:

* Data interpretation
* Research analysis
* Root cause analysis
* System investigation
* Technical problem diagnosis

Primary Challenge:
Complexity and information overload.

---

TASK TYPE: Generative

Description:
Work focused on producing new ideas, drafts, or possibilities.

Generative tasks emphasize exploration, variation, and creative output.

Typical Examples:

* Idea generation
* Draft writing
* Concept exploration
* Brainstorming
* Content creation

Primary Challenge:
Avoiding shallow or repetitive outputs.

---

TASK TYPE: Evaluative

Description:
Work focused on judging quality, comparing options, and identifying
strengths and weaknesses.

Evaluative tasks require criteria, critical thinking, and structured
assessment.

Typical Examples:

* Reviewing proposals
* Critiquing designs
* Comparing alternatives
* Quality assessment
* Risk evaluation

Primary Challenge:
Bias, incomplete criteria, and inconsistent judgment.

---

TASK TYPE: Reduction

Description:
Work focused on simplifying, summarizing, or extracting key information
from larger sources.

Reduction tasks transform complexity into clarity.

Typical Examples:

* Summarizing documents
* Extracting key insights
* Creating executive summaries
* Condensing research
* Simplifying explanations

Primary Challenge:
Preserving meaning while reducing volume.
